About Sam Altman

A Visionary Leader in Technology

Sam Altman is a prominent American entrepreneur, investor, and the CEO of OpenAI. He has established himself as a leading figure in the artificial intelligence boom, driving innovation and shaping the future of technology.

With a keen eye for transformative ideas and a passion for technological advancement, Altman has become one of the most influential voices in Silicon Valley and the global tech community.

Named one of Time Magazine's 100 most influential people in 2023, Altman continues to push the boundaries of what's possible in AI while advocating for responsible development and deployment of these powerful technologies.

Key Roles

Y Combinator

As president of Y Combinator from 2014 to 2019, Sam Altman transformed the startup accelerator into a global powerhouse. Under his leadership, Y Combinator helped launch and nurture some of today's most successful companies, including Airbnb, Dropbox, Stripe, and Reddit.

During his tenure, he expanded Y Combinator's reach and impact, increasing the number of startups funded per batch and establishing programs to support founders from underrepresented backgrounds.

OpenAI

As CEO of OpenAI, Sam Altman has overseen the development of groundbreaking AI technologies like ChatGPT, GPT-4, and DALL-E. Under his leadership, OpenAI has become a pioneer in the field of artificial intelligence, creating systems with increasingly sophisticated capabilities.

Altman has guided OpenAI through its transition from a non-profit to a "capped-profit" company, securing major investments while maintaining the organization's mission to ensure that artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity.
